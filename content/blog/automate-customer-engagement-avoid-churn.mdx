Today's customers are demanding, digital-first individuals not settling for anything less than a frictionless experience. To keep up with their changing needs, organisations must focus on streamlining their customer engagement processes as they aim to retain their existing customers.

Keeping your customers happy and engaged requires a significant amount of time, energy, and resources, no matter the size of your customer base. That's where customer engagement automation comes in. About [40% of customer interactions will be automated](https://www.superoffice.com/blog/customer-experience-statistics/) as the business landscape edges toward growing adoption of automated tools to manage every step of the customer journey.

In this article, we provide deeper insights into the customer engagement process, why it's important to automate customer engagement, and the automation tools you need to deploy to reduce manual tasks.

## What is Customer Engagement?

Customer engagement refers to how you interact with your customers from the moment they sign up for your product or service until their relationship with your brand ends. The idea behind the customer engagement process is that keeping in touch with existing customers should provide a seamless experience and boost their trust in your business. This way, you can worry less about the high costs of acquiring a new customer.

The customer engagement process is not just about selling products; it's also about establishing everlasting relationships with customers on a personal level. They help you understand their behaviour and preferences in your mutually beneficial relationship so you can cater to them better in the future.

> **Pro Tip:** To establish a strong relationship with your customers, you must have an idea of who they are, what they like, and what drives their buying decisions. This also helps you identify what type of content they will enjoy and how best to communicate with them more often.

## Why is Customer Engagement Important?

Customer engagement is not just about keeping the sales numbers at an all-time high - although that is the end goal. Whether you have one customer or a thousand, keeping them happy, their questions answered, and their problems solved will go a long way in keeping them loyal to your business.

- **To know your customer better:** Engaging with your customers allows you to better understand their needs and requirements. You'll better understand what challenges and obstacles your customers face and offer solutions through your products and services.
- **To spot trends and discover new opportunities:** When actively engaging with your customers, you'll be able to identify emerging (and outdated) trends in your industry. This will allow you to prepare for the future and even take advantage of new opportunities.
- **To increase customer retention rate:** Engaging with your customers is a start to identifying and resolving any issues they might have with your products or services. Being proactive and taking note of your customers' needs, expectations, and preferences will allow you to see what issues they face and respond quickly.
- **To build trust with your customers:** One of the best ways to build trust is by giving them a voice. Providing your customers with an opportunity to give feedback and share their thoughts on your offerings will show them that you're actively listening.
- **To understand the value of specific products or services:** When actively engaging with your customers, you can ask them to provide their thoughts on your products and services. This will allow you to better understand how your customers value your offerings and why.

The customer engagement process, if done well, can save you a lot of time and money in the long term, thanks to increased customer lifetime value. And the more you automate customer engagement, the less time you'll spend on manual tasks.

## Reasons to Automate Customer Engagement

There's much to consider when executing a successful customer engagement strategy. From setting up the right user experience for your customers to keeping track of how you engage with them - there's just too much to keep in mind.

Automation reduces the manual workload by using machines or computer software that operates automatically rather than requiring human intervention. Business processes can be automated by adopting artificial intelligence, machine learning algorithms, and other advanced technologies.

- **Reduce manual tasks** - Automation helps you reduce recurring manual tasks done regularly.
- **More time to focus on important, higher-value tasks** - Automating customer engagement means less time spent on tedious customer service tasks, manual lead generation, and other tasks better suited for computer automation.
- **Get more done** - Automation frees up time for creating content, reaching out to new customers, monitoring your campaigns, and edging closer to your goals.
- **Save money** - Acquiring a new customer can cost five times more than retaining an existing one. Automating customer engagement keeps you in touch with your customers more efficiently and ensures their needs are always handled.

### Benefits of Customer Engagement Automation

Customer engagement automation enables marketers to measure customer loyalty and see where improvements must be made. With the right tools in place, your business can achieve peak performance by streamlining customer engagement activities.

- **Increased lifetime value:** By keeping your customers happy, you maintain their lifetime value since they'll stick around for more of your goods and services.
- **Better brand reputation:** Customers are more likely to share positive experiences with brands they like - brands that dedicate their time to their needs.
- **Reduced churn rates:** By automating customer engagement, answering your customer's questions, and solving their problems faster and more efficiently, you are more likely to retain customers.
- **More efficient tracking of customer behaviour:** Customer engagement automation allows you to track and measure customer behaviour in real-time - so you can see how customers interact with your brand. You can track how often a customer [has been in contact with your sales and marketing team](/blog/sales-automation-tips-use-cases), what they're enquiring about, and their level of satisfaction.
- **Optimise your marketing campaigns:** Customer engagement automation allows you to capture customer information so that you can [create targeted marketing campaigns](/blog/pipedrive-apps-marketing-automation). This ensures that your marketing is reaching the right people.

Customer engagement automation also allows you to monitor **key performance indicators (KPIs)** such as customer retention, customer acquisition, and the customer journey. This will help you identify areas needing improvement and create strategies to fix them.

## What Parts of Customer Engagement Can Be Automated and How?

A healthy amount of customer engagement automation is essential, but it can quickly turn into an unhealthy practice if you don't know when to draw the line. Some activities, such as one-on-one communications via social media and other channels, still need human intervention.

- **Lead nurturing:** Lead nurturing is engaging with potential customers to increase their likelihood of becoming customers and remaining loyal to your brand. [Email marketing](/blog/pipedrive-apps-marketing-automation), SMS messages, content marketing, or push notifications at every funnel stage. For instance, a sales CRM can automatically send a welcome email to a new user.
- **Retargeting:** Retargeting is a method of advertising that shows ads to people who have previously visited your website or brand. Retargeting ads can be triggered by several events, such as visiting a page on your site, searching for a specific product, or even leaving the site without purchasing anything.
- **Automated email campaigns:** Automated email campaigns can be used at any point in the funnel, depending on the message you're trying to send. Welcome emails for new customers, win-back campaigns for lapsed customers, and onboarding campaigns for anyone who has never interacted with your customer service team.
