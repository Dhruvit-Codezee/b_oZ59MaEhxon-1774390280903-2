## What Is Lead Generation?

Lead generation attracts strangers and converts them into someone interested in your company's product or service. It involves emails, events, mailings, blog posts, and other materials that spread the word about your brand. Every company leverages some form of lead generation to acquire customers.

## What Is Lead Generation Automation?

There are different options when it comes to generating leads for your business. It could be through social media, email marketing, referrals, etc. Automating any of these tasks gives you the upper hand as you can do the same thing with less effort and more efficiency - this is what lead gen automation is all about.

Lead generation automation refers to software programs that automate manual tasks for generating leads for your business. It requires setting up lead generation systems that target relevant prospects, capture their contact information, and deliver this information to salespeople who can nurture them into qualified leads.

Lead-gen is important because it turns strangers into qualified prospects more likely to buy from you. The more leads you have in your sales funnel, the greater your chance of making a sale. About [80% of marketers](https://www.invespcro.com/blog/marketing-automation/) who use lead gen automation see an increase in the number of leads generated, with 77% recording an increase in conversion.

Lead generation automation aims to help you capture as many high-quality leads as possible at the lowest cost per lead. Even though implementing lead automation software requires investment, it starts to pay off soon. Your operations become more efficient, and you can do things you would have never done manually, like lead scoring.

### Processes and Workflows Pipedrive Can Automate

Automation can save you time and money and increase your business's efficiency. Consider automating these lead generation processes using Pipedrive:

#### Your Pipeline

In Pipedrive, you create a pipeline for every type of deal that you want to track. For example, if you have different products or services, each can have its pipeline. A pipeline shows all stages using visual representation from the initial contact with the lead to closing the deal.

Pipedrive allows you to apply coloured cues to your pipelines so that you know what to do when a deal reaches a particular stage. For example, if a deal reaches a stage where it's almost complete, the colour reminds you to contact the customer.

#### Your Emails

Email management is the biggest time-waster in the modern workplace. However, the problem with emails is that you cannot avoid them.

But what if you could save time on email processing without skipping it altogether? That's what email automation is all about. In Pipedrive, you can automatically send emails when a deal reaches a particular stage in your pipeline or when the task is due.

For example, if you're trying to close more deals, one of the best ways is to follow up with prospects who haven't gotten back to you yet. You can send them automated emails based on their latest activity or inactivity.

#### Your Activities

Organising calls, meetings, follow-ups, and tasks are part of the lead generation process. But even with these daily routines, there's room for automation!

You can automate the following activities:

- Email follow-ups
- Sales call preparation
- Sales meeting preparation
- Follow-up calls and emails
- Messages
- Deals' status updates
- Customer data collection
- Information updates
- Reporting

#### Your Deal Progress

Never miss a chance of closing a deal with automatic reminders, so your team can sell faster than ever.

You can automate the process of moving deals through your sales pipeline. Every time a deal reaches a new stage, Pipedrive can let you and your team know about it, keeping everyone in the loop on time. It gives you a clear view of what is happening in your sales cycle and where things are slowing down or speeding up. You can even use this information to send emails to your customers according to their stage in your sales pipeline.

### Setting Automation Goals Based on Your Overall Lead Gen Strategy

Another critical step to creating a lead generation workflow is to decide your end goal. This will guide you on what processes to automate and how.

If you're looking for a quick way to get leads from people who've already engaged with your brand, a more direct approach would be best. For example, you can send an email immediately after someone fills out a form for a free trial of your product or service.

On the other hand, if you're targeting someone who isn't familiar with your brand yet or has minimal engagement (like someone who downloaded one of your ebooks), you'll need to take it slow and nurture them through the sales funnel. You can start by sending them emails with relevant content until they are ready to convert.

### Metrics for Pipedrive Lead-Gen Automation

You have a ton of data at your fingertips, thanks to Pipedrive. But what are the key metrics that you should be tracking to measure the success of your Pipedrive lead generation strategies?

You should know:

1. How many leads did I get?
2. How many of those leads turned into sales?
3. How much money did I make from those sales?
4. Where did the best leads come from? Which of your channels are the most effective?
5. Which automation workflows were most effective at turning prospects into customers?

Tracking the right metrics will help you know whether your lead gen automation efforts in Pipedrive are paying off.

### Prioritisation

Automation is gradual, so don't expect to automate everything immediately. Hence, you must decide which operations are the most critical for your business and must be optimised first.

Prioritisation of tasks allows the creation of an automation strategy with clear stages and timelines. You know what to do next and go step by step.

### Lessons Learned and Improvements

The most important thing about lead gen automation is that it's not a set-it-and-forget-it tool. Marketing automation workflows are like any other marketing tactic. You have to test and improve them over time.

If something's not working or the numbers are poor, fine-tune your process until it runs smoothly. Learn from the results you are getting and make tweaks where necessary.
