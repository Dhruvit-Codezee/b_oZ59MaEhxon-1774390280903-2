Countless SMEs, large enterprises and multinational corporations use marketing automation to streamline their marketing efforts and improve efficiency. Automating your marketing processes saves time and frees resources that could be better spent elsewhere - allowing you to focus on growing your business.

If you're new to the world of marketing automation and you're unsure of where to start, then we're here to help.

## What is marketing automation?

First, what is marketing automation? Essentially, the term refers to the process of using software to automate marketing activities. This includes, but is not limited to:

- Email marketing
- Social media marketing
- Mobile messaging
- Advertising management
- Data collection
- Content marketing
- Prospecting

The best marketing automation software helps to bring all or most of these processes together on one platform. This offers a wealth of benefits for businesses by improving productivity, driving increased sales, and often reducing costs.

Most importantly, marketing automation allows you to reach a new audience and then provide them with a personalised online experience that can help move them further down the sales funnel.

## How does marketing automation work?

Marketing automation combines sophisticated software and sometimes artificial intelligence (AI) to create a platform that carries out the bulk of your repetitive marketing tasks for you. No matter whether you want to streamline your prospecting process or you want to make sure you're targeting the right people, marketing automation can always help.

Some of the best marketing automation platforms use cutting-edge tools such as integrated buyer intent data and social retargeting to ensure your marketing efforts make the biggest impact. In many cases, marketing automation platforms can offer tiered price plans depending on how many contacts you have or how many emails you want to send.

Some price plans are tiered according to the features you want to use for your business, so always ensure you understand what features you need before considering an investment. This is where a [marketing automation consultant](/services) like us may be able to help you.

## Why do you need marketing automation?

No matter what sector you're in, all businesses can benefit from automating their marketing processes. If you're unsure of how it can help you, we've listed some of the main marketing automation benefits below.

### It can save you time

One of the core benefits of having an automated marketing system is that it can help you save a significant amount of time, and business time is money.

Prospecting, email marketing, retargeting and onboarding are not complicated processes, but they can be extremely time-consuming. Implementing an automation system takes these jobs off your hands, giving you more time to focus on other important business tasks.

### It helps to align your sales and marketing goals

It's easier for sales and marketing departments to work cohesively with the right automation platform. Using one all-encompassing platform helps to foster communication between these departments. This helps everyone stay on track to achieve the same shared goals.

### It can help to increase your ROI

Using marketing automation AI, software, and buyer intent data can help you extract the maximum value from your marketing efforts. With the right automation software, you can make your business more efficient, reduce costs and increase your ROI over time.

### It offers a multi-channel view of prospect behaviour

When working with multiple software and platforms, getting a clear picture of how your prospects interact and behave can be difficult. Whereas the best marketing automation software can bring all these processes together in one place. This additional data feeds into better performance and results.

This way, you can see exactly what is and isn't working with your marketing efforts and focus on what works for you.

## What does a marketing automation campaign look like?

No two marketing automation campaigns are the same; every organisation will have different goals and aspirations. However, in general, most businesses want to get more leads, more customers and increase sales, revenue and profit - right?

Fortunately, implementing an effective marketing automation campaign can help you to do just that. We've compiled a quick guide below to help you understand what your outbound marketing campaign could look like.

### Automated prospecting

Assuming you're starting from scratch and don't know who you want to target, your marketing campaign will likely start with prospecting.

This process can be automated using sophisticated buyer intent data. Buyer intent data uses AI to scan the web for pre-determined buying signals and identify the relevant contact details. Marketing automation software using buyer intent can scan social media and crawl open-source sites to identify businesses engaging with your competitors and keywords and are, therefore, more likely to buy from you. This data can then be fed into your outbound marketing campaigns.

This is a more targeted approach to prospecting that will help you to minimise wasted resources. Our recommended automation software can also find the email addresses of decision-makers at these companies, transforming your sales team's processes and freeing up significant time.

Once you've identified your list of prospects, it's time to create demand for your product or service. Marketing automation software can help you to do this by:

1. Creating multi-channel automated marketing campaigns
2. Creating automated buyer journeys
3. Creating lead magnets such as downloadable eBooks, pop-ups, forms and live chat integrations

This tried and tested approach will help you ensure your marketing efforts get in front of the right people at the right time.

### Nurturing your leads

So, you've got your prospects and warmed them up, so what's next? At this stage, it's essential to nurture your leads so that they stay warm, convert and become regular customers. Fortunately, marketing automation can help with this process too.

You can automate your retargeting, email marketing, social media marketing and more to ensure that your brand stays at the forefront of your client's minds. When combined with automated personalisation (different prospects presented with different content), you have a powerful tool that will help to increase your chances of securing a regular client base.

### Sales automation

Marketing automation is useless if it doesn't automatically deliver leads to your sales team. On average, a sales rep spends just 35% of their time selling - the solution? Automate those mundane and repetitive tasks.

The best automation software will also allow your sales team to track website visitors, manage sales journeys, create email templates and more. This will help you to streamline your sales process and maximise your conversions in the final stage of your campaign.
