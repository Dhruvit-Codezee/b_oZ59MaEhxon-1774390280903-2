## How Long Does it Take Your Sales Department to Sign Documents?

Salespeople spend roughly [half an hour](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/sales-paperwork-do-we-really-need-andr%C3%A9-ferreira/) on administrative work and 7.6% of their time on paperwork. Of course, this will depend on the business's nature, the team's size, and any other agreements and conditions. Here's a brief glimpse of how much time different documents take to process in the sales department:

### Approving and Signing a Sales Contract

A sales contract marks an agreement between a buyer and seller and must be signed to put it into legal effect. There are many departments and stakeholders involved in the negotiation process. As a result, getting the contract signed by all the stakeholders can take [4-6 weeks](https://health.uconn.edu/procurement-operations-contracts/contracts/contract-process/) to get it ready for execution.

### Approving and Signing a Proposal

The negotiations and proposal process can take weeks, if not months (or even years in some cases) before a client signs a proposal. If you're in sales, you must invest much time in the proposal process. You need to understand your client's needs, and you need to understand what role your company's product or service offerings will play in their needs.

### Signing NDAs

NDAs protect trade secrets and confidential property. If you have a big company or are in a highly competitive field, you almost definitely have NDAs on the books with your clients. These documents can take [30 days](https://www.techtransfer.iastate.edu/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/2.-Non-Disclosure-Agreement.pdf), weeks, months, or even years to sign.

Most sales contracts and proposals take weeks or months to get approved and signed off on. This is especially true if your sales department uses a template for each document type. Smart salespeople know that the longer you wait, the bigger the opportunity for the competitor to strike and the higher the chances of losing the deal. And that's where automated sales document tracking and signing come in.
